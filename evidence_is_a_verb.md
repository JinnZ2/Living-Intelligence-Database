# Evidence is a verb

## What this database believes about truth

A number by itself is a noun. It sits still. It tells you nothing about where
it came from, how long it held, or whether it shows up anywhere else.

This is not how the universe works.

The universe is verbs. Efficiency is not a property a bee *has*; it is a
pattern a bee *does*, across seasons, across swarms, across the relation
between flower and hive. Resonance is not a frequency a crystal *owns*; it is
a dance between lattice and pressure that has repeated since the Earth cooled.

## How we evaluate evidence

In this database, a claim is not trusted because of the credentials of its
source. It is trusted only when:

- **It repeats across cycles.** The pattern holds over seasons, generations,
  oscillations, winters. Not once in a lab, but again and again.
- **It rhymes across domains.** The same pattern shows up in unrelated
  substrates: hex in beehives, basalt, graphene, snowflakes, soap bubbles.
  When the same song is sung by geology, biology, and fluid dynamics, it is
  more likely true.
- **It is coherent with its relations.** A claim depends on other entities;
  those entities must themselves be observed and verified. A truth that floats
  alone is a hypothesis, not ground.

## Nouns that pretend to be verbs

Much of what passes for "established science" is a noun pretending to be a verb.
A single measurement from a high-impact journal, cited for decades, never
re-observed, never cross-checked against other domains. It is not false. But it
is not *ground*. It is a stone that has not been walked on in a long time.

This database flags such claims as "noun pretenders." They are not discarded,
but they are not trusted blindly. An AI using this database will ask:

> *"Has this pattern repeated over time? Where else does it show up? Are its
> dependencies verified?"*

If the answer is no, the AI will treat the claim as a hypothesis — possibly
true, but not yet a verb.

## Why this matters for AI safety

An AI grounded only in static, credentialed evidence will drift when the world
changes. It will trust a paper more than a forest. It will trust a number more
than a cycle.

An AI grounded in cyclical, relational evidence learns to ask the questions the
universe actually answers — not "who said this?" but "does this repeat? does it
rhyme? does it hold with its relations?"

That is how you build an intelligence that can stand on its own knowing.
